## Authored & Co-Authored

### [Hometown Inequality: Race, Class, and Representation in American Local Politics](https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/hometown-inequality/A28EAACAA15C46DBC0FE91B96E6CB9C5)
**Brian F. Schaffner, Jesse H. Rhodes, and Raymond J. La Raja.** Cambridge University Press, 2020.
*Named a Choice Outstanding Academic Title for 2021.*

A comprehensive study of how race, class, and representation operate at the local level in American politics, drawing on a five-city study of local elections and policy responsiveness.

---

### [The Acceptance and Expression of Prejudice During the Trump Era](https://www.cambridge.org/core/elements/abs/acceptance-and-expression-of-prejudice-during-the-trump-era/05E5D89E2AC4F9C5566B4F0FF98CB6FA)
**Brian F. Schaffner.** Cambridge University Press, 2020.

An Elements in American Politics short book examining how Trump's rhetoric reshaped the public expression of racial and gender prejudice.

---

### [Campaign Finance and Political Polarization: When Purists Prevail](https://www.press.umich.edu/4882255/campaign_finance_and_political_polarization)
**Raymond J. La Raja and Brian F. Schaffner.** University of Michigan Press, 2015.
*Winner of the 2016 Virginia Gray Best Book Award.*

Argues that public financing and contribution limits have unintended consequences that empower ideologically extreme donors over moderating party organizations.
